Justin's Design Principles

Key Principles

  • Visibility
  • Consistency
  • Feedback
  • Constraints
  • Affordances
  • Accessibility

Complexity Considerations

  • Be as easy as email
  • Allow users to learn by doing
  • Provide fluid pathways
  • Reduce clutter

Cultural/Gender Considerations

  • Consider differences in reading habits - left to right or right to left
  • Consider differences in offensive language or gestures
  • Don't assume cultural similarity just because of geographical location
  • Consider if gendered language is helpful or harmful

Conceptual Considerations

  • Use mind maps to visually structure ideas
  • Use prototypes to get a feel for your system
  • Use personas to build scenarios, scenarios to build stories, and stories to build features
  • Create use case diagrams to define your system's requirements and goals
  • Explain your system using effective metaphors

Human-Centered and Cognitive Considerations

  • Adapt your tools to users - don't expect vice versa
  • Present your information to be processed in bite-size chunks (progressive disclosure)
  • Take advantage of progressive disclosure
    • Use links, dropdown menus, sliders, and categorization

Emotional Considerations

  • Be empathetic
  • Design for security
  • Surprise your users
  • Don't underestimate the power of persuasion

Accessibility

  • Perceivable, operable, understandable, robust
  • "Humans might fail - but they are not wrong"
  • Frustrating + difficult = inaccessible
  • Consistency is key

Mobile Design

  • Make your design scalable (zooming and different screen sizes)
  • Design for one-handed use
    • Keep important buttons in the bottom half of the screen
    • Center your buttons horizontally - don't put them close to the edges

Interface Considerations

  • Build for scanning, not reading
  • Take advantage of conventions
  • Create effective visual hierarchies
  • Break pages into clearly defined areas
  • Avoid "happy talk" instructions
